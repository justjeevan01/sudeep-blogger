Blog #19

Blockchain Technology

Distributed Systems Last updated: April 2024

What is Blockchain?

Distributed ledger technology using cryptography to create immutable record of transactions across decentralized network.

Key Characteristics

  • Decentralized: No single point of control
  • Immutable: Records cannot be altered
  • Transparent: All transactions visible to network
  • Secure: Cryptographic hashing protects data

Blockchain Structure

Blocks: Contains transactions and reference to previous block

Hash: Unique identifier derived from block contents

Merkle Tree: Efficient way to summarize and verify transactions

Consensus Mechanisms

Proof of Work (PoW)

Miners solve complex puzzles to validate transactions. Energy-intensive but highly secure.

Proof of Stake (PoS)

Validators chosen based on their stake. More energy-efficient.

Other Mechanisms

Delegated Proof of Stake, Proof of Authority, Proof of Elapsed Time

Smart Contracts

Self-executing contracts on blockchain with terms written in code.

  • Ethereum: Most popular smart contract platform (Solidity)
  • Hyperledger: Enterprise blockchain framework
  • Polkadot: Multi-chain blockchain platform

Cryptocurrency

Bitcoin: First cryptocurrency, peer-to-peer payment

Ethereum: Platform for decentralized applications

Altcoins: Alternative cryptocurrencies (Litecoin, Ripple, etc.)

DApps (Decentralized Applications)

Applications running on blockchain network:

  • Finance (DeFi) - lending, trading, payments
  • Gaming - ownership of digital assets
  • Supply chain - transparency and traceability
  • Identity - self-sovereign identity

Blockchain Use Cases

  • Financial services and remittance
  • Supply chain management
  • Healthcare records
  • Voting systems
  • Intellectual property and royalties

Challenges

  • Scalability - limited transaction throughput
  • Energy consumption
  • Regulatory uncertainty
  • Security vulnerabilities
Tip: Blockchain technology is evolving rapidly - stay informed about latest developments and layer 2 solutions.
